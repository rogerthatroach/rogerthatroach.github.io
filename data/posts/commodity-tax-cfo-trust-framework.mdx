In September 2022, I joined <Glossed>CFO Group</Glossed> at a major Canadian bank as a Senior Data Scientist. No one had asked me to build AI. I was hired to help automate the <Glossed>Commodity Tax</Glossed> return process — a manual workflow that consumed finance analysts for months per cycle.

By Q4 2023, the process took **90 minutes**. The metric earned a CFO Quarterly Team Award. More importantly, it earned the credibility to propose Aegis, Astraeus, and eventually Prometheus — a cascade that shaped the next three years.

This post walks through the architectural decision that made it land, the constraints that shaped it, and what I&rsquo;d do differently.

## Context

Commodity tax returns at a major Canadian bank are not small paperwork. Finance teams pull transactions from General Ledger journals — millions of rows per cycle, covering **~$250M GST + ~$350M PVAT** in allocated tax liability — reconcile them against tax categories, and produce returns that reach CFO-level sign-off.

The process had accumulated years of institutional knowledge: undocumented edge cases, manual overrides, tribal understanding of which GL accounts mapped to which tax buckets.

Automating it wasn&rsquo;t &ldquo;wrap a Python script around Excel.&rdquo; It meant codifying tribal knowledge without breaking anyone&rsquo;s trust in the numbers.

## First project, first audition

When you join an organization as the AI person and your first assignment is process automation, there are two ways to read it. You can treat it as a detour — &ldquo;I&rsquo;ll do this so I can get to the real AI work.&rdquo; Or you can treat it as an audition.

I treated it as an audition. Finance leaders were not going to green-light a machine-learning platform for CFO-level decisions on the strength of my CV. They were going to green-light it on the strength of my first delivery.

That framing changed how I built.

## What this opened

By the time Commodity Tax was running cleanly, the permission structure inside the CFO Group had shifted. I could propose <Glossed>Aegis v1</Glossed> — a benchmarking engine for the Big 6 Canadian banks — and get the air cover to build it. When Aegis v1 shipped (2025 CFO One RBC Team Award), I could propose <Glossed>Aegis v2</Glossed>, the text-to-SQL refactor, and deliver it in a concurrent 2-week sprint while Astraeus productionization was underway. When <Glossed>Astraeus</Glossed> reached production scale, I had the credibility to conceive <Glossed>Prometheus</Glossed> from an intern&rsquo;s proof-of-concept and scale it bank-wide.

Every one of those projects was technically ambitious. None of them would have been greenlit by a new hire with nothing to show for it. Commodity Tax was underwriting every subsequent bet.

## What I&rsquo;d do differently

One scope call I&rsquo;d revisit: the first dashboard set deliberately did NOT surface the category-mapping rule book. Analysts could drill from final number → journal row, but the rules that produced each mapping lived in Python code. When new categories emerged (tax law changes, new GL accounts), only I could add them.

In retrospect, I&rsquo;d have built a <em>mapping-rules dashboard</em> from cycle 2 onward — letting analysts propose rule additions directly (still requiring my code-review approval before production), turning the mapping layer from a single-person bottleneck into a shared surface. It would have cost ~1 extra week in v1 and saved much more than that across the next two years.

## The pattern

Your first project at a new organization isn&rsquo;t just a deliverable. It&rsquo;s the audition that determines which doors open next.

The architectural decision — Tableau as a transparency layer, not an output — was the first time I applied a principle I&rsquo;d use in every subsequent system: **the hardest thing in production AI isn&rsquo;t getting the answer right; it&rsquo;s getting the humans who own the decision to trust the system that produced it.**

Astraeus&rsquo;s GPT-routes-intent / deterministic-code-handles-truth separation is a descendant of this idea. Prometheus&rsquo;s MCP tool contracts that log every field assignment to an audit trail are a descendant of this idea. In every production AI system at a regulated institution, the architecture has to survive stakeholder audit.

Commodity Tax was where I learned that.

The technology changes. The pattern doesn&rsquo;t.
