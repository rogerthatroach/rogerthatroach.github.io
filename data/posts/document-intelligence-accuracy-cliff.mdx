At Quantiphi, I led the Document Intelligence work for Humana &mdash; an insurance claims processing pipeline where document verification accuracy went from ~70% with Google Cloud Document AI alone to 99.95% after a layered hybrid approach.

The engineering that got there is one story. What I actually learned &mdash; the part that shaped every RAG pipeline I built at RBC three years later &mdash; is a different one.

## The accuracy cliff

## The hybrid stack

{/* TODO: Describe the three-layer approach and why each layer earned
its place.
- Document AI OCR as the base layer &mdash; cheap, good, not specific enough
- OpenCV for pixel-level checkbox detection &mdash; targeted, deterministic
- Random Forest classifier on top &mdash; learns the edge cases
- Vertex AI for deployment; BigTable + BigQuery for ingestion at scale

The question the reader wants answered: why this layered approach
instead of training one bigger model? Cost-per-inference. Document
AI is fast and cheap; custom models are slow and expensive. The
architecture routes documents to the right layer based on what
they need.
*/}
